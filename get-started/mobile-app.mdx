The mobile app is what you actually use on race day. It runs the plan you built on the web — pacing, fueling, gear, checkpoints, alerts — fully offline.

## Install

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="iOS">
    Search **Absurd Running** in the App Store, or open the link from absurdrunning.com on your iPhone and tap **Get**.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Android">
    Search **Absurd Running** in the Play Store, or open the link from absurdrunning.com on your Android and tap **Install**.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Sign in

Use the same account you signed up with on the web. Your routes and plans sync automatically.

## Download a plan for race day

Plans run online by default, but you'll want to download them for race day so they keep working without signal.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the plan">
    From the **Plans** tab, tap the plan you want to run.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Download">
    The plan details + map tiles for the route get saved locally to your phone. Map tile download takes longer than the plan itself — start it before you leave the house.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify offline">
    Toggle airplane mode on. Open the plan again — everything still works. Toggle airplane mode off.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## On race day

- Open the plan when you start running.
- The app shows you which segment you're on, your target pace, what to eat, what to wear at the next checkpoint, and what's in your drop bag.
- Per-checkpoint alerts fire as you approach each one.
- No notifications repeat — if you miss one, you'll see it in the timeline.

## Things to know

- **Battery**: keep your phone in low-power mode and screen brightness low. The map view is the biggest power draw.
- **Watch integration**: Apple Watch and Garmin support is in progress. For now, alerts come through your phone.
- **Updates**: keep the app on the latest version before race day. Updates ship frequently and the plan format may evolve.
