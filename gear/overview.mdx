The **Gear library** is your reusable kit. Anything you might race with lives here once — apparel, footwear, packs, lights, nutrition — and you attach the right items to the right checkpoints when you build a plan.

If you race more than one ultra a year, this is where you'll save the most time. Build your gear list once, reach for the same items across every plan.

## What goes in here

Five built-in categories cover most ultras:

- **Apparel** — shirts, jackets, shorts, tights, gloves, buffs.
- **Footwear** — shoes for each terrain, spare laces, gaiters.
- **Electronics** — watch, headlamp, backup light, charging cable, power bank.
- **Equipment** — pack, vest, poles, hydration bladder, soft flasks.
- **Fueling** — gels, bars, drink mix, salt, real food.

Each item can have a photo, a weight, a quantity, and a note.

## Attach to checkpoints

The real value happens inside a plan. On the **Gear** step of the planner, you assign items to checkpoints:

- What's on you at the **start** — race kit, shoes, headlamp.
- What you **swap** at an aid station — fresh shirt, new socks, jacket on.
- What's **waiting in a drop bag** — spare shoes, full battery pack, more fuel.

Once assigned, the mobile app reminds you at each checkpoint what to grab, swap, or pack.

## Linked products

If you're an athlete brand or partner, gear items can link to a product page. Useful for sponsored races where the organizer wants athletes to see specific brands at each aid station.
