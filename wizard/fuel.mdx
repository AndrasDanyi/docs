Fueling well is the difference between a finish and a DNF in most ultras. The Fuel step turns abstract goals ("I want to eat 75 g/h of carbs") into a concrete plan ("1 Maurten gel every 45 minutes from start to finish") that the mobile app fires as reminders on race day.

The step has two halves: **Fueling Targets** (the hourly numbers, scaled for conditions and effort) and **Fueling Strategy** (the actual rules and items that deliver them). Iterate between the two: set targets, plan rules, check whether actuals match, refine.

## Fueling Targets

### Weather

Conditions you expect on race day. Drives sweat-rate scaling, fluid + sodium targets, and per-segment temperature-adjusted intake.

- **Constant Weather** — manual pick from a temperature band (cold / cool / mild / warm / hot / very hot). Use when no forecast is available or when you want a fixed worst-case scenario.
- **Weather Forecast** — pulls the actual hour-by-hour forecast for the race date set in Plan Details. Feeds segment-by-segment temperatures across the day.

### Body Metrics

Per-runner inputs that scale the targets:

- **Body weight** — drives the per-kg components of carb and fluid targets.
- **Sweat rate** — litres of fluid lost per hour at race effort. Default ~1 L/h mild, up to 1.4 L/h hot. Scales with the Weather temperature band.
- **Sweat sodium concentration** — milligrams per litre. Default ~800 mg/L; salty sweaters run 1,200+ mg/L.
- **GI tolerance** — bounds the carbs-per-hour ceiling. Trained guts can push past 90 g/h; untrained stomachs cap closer to 60.

Defaults come from your [Plan Defaults](/get-started/first-plan#settings). Override per plan when conditions or your race form demand it.

### Fueling Targets (the hourly numbers)

The headline values, derived from Weather + Body Metrics:

- **Carbs per hour** — 60–100 g/h for ultra distance with a trained gut.
- **Fluid per hour** — 400–800 ml depending on conditions and sweat rate.
- **Sodium per hour** — 300–800 mg, higher for hot conditions and salty sweaters.
- **Calories per hour** — 200–400 cal/h.
- **Caffeine per hour** — optional, capped at safe daily totals.

### Segment Targets

Per-segment adjustments based on the route's terrain and the weather forecast. Hot exposed segments need more fluid; technical climbs need fewer gels because you can't open them while scrambling. The planner suggests adjustments automatically, and you can tweak each segment's targets manually.

## Fueling Strategy

The execution half. Targets tell you *what* you need; Strategy is *how* you'll deliver it.

The chart shows every planned intake along the course distance. Below it, a list lets you add and edit rules and items. A totals row compares your actuals against the targets so you can spot under- or over-shoot at a glance.

### Fuel Rules vs. Fuel Items

- **Fuel Rule** — a REPEATING intake. Same item, same schedule across a window. "1 gel every 45 minutes from start to finish" → ~16 reminders across a 12-hour race.
- **Fuel Item** — a ONE-OFF intake. Single scheduled event, no repeat. "100 mg caffeine pill at minute 360" → one reminder when fatigue is about to bite.

### Configuring a rule

- **Frequency** — every (recurring), at (single trigger), or at every checkpoint.
- **Measure type** — time (every 45 min), distance (every 5 km), or checkpoint (at every aid station that offers gels).
- **From / To** — bound the rule to a window. Useful for starting fueling later (let your stomach settle), stopping earlier (last 30 min before finish), or restricting to a specific segment.
- **Consume with (chaser)** — optional companion item. Take a gel with 200 ml of water; salt cap with 250 ml fluid. The mobile app surfaces both in one reminder.
- **Portion size / Split mode** — log fractions (½ bar) or spread a dose evenly through a window (a sandwich in 4 bites across km 60–80).

### Per-segment actuals vs. targets

Each segment shows its own actual totals from your rules + items compared against Segment Targets. Green = on plan; red = under or over for that segment. Click a flagged segment to jump to its per-segment view and refine.

### Checkpoint refill suggestions

When a checkpoint has matching offerings (water, cola, gels) declared on the [route checkpoints](/routes/checkpoints), the planner suggests refills there instead of asking you to carry everything from the start. Refills surface inline with your rules — they're not a separate editor, just a recommendation you can accept by adding the matching rule.

### The iteration loop

Targets → Rules + Items → Actuals → compare → adjust. Most plans take 2–3 passes:

1. Set a coarse rule ("1 gel every 45 min").
2. Read actuals — say carbs come in at 50 g/h against a 75 g/h target.
3. Layer items into the gaps ("Cola at every aid station").
4. Re-read; actuals land around 70 g/h.
5. Bump gel quantity to 1.5 → on target.

## What becomes a mobile-app reminder

Every rule and every item turns into a scheduled notification on race day. Each reminder shows the item, quantity, and any chaser. Acting on it marks it consumed; ignoring it logs a miss. The actuals you tuned against in Fueling Strategy are exactly what gets delivered.

<Tip>
  Don't go above your trained intake on race day. The plan is only as good as what your gut can handle — test the targets on long training runs first.
</Tip>
