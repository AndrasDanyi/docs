Fueling well is the difference between a finish and a DNF in most ultras. This step turns abstract goals ("I want to eat 250 cal/hr") into a concrete plan ("at km 32 I'll grab two gels and refill both flasks").

## Targets

Four sliders set your per-hour intake targets:

- **Calories per hour** — typical ultra range is 200–400 cal/hr.
- **Carbohydrates per hour** — 60–100g for trained guts.
- **Fluid per hour** — 400–800ml depending on conditions and sweat rate.
- **Sodium per hour** — 300–800mg, higher for hot conditions.

The defaults are conservative for ultra distance and assume an average runner. If you've trained your gut to higher carbs (90+ g/hr), pull the sliders up.

## Strategy

How intake is distributed across the race:

- **Even** — same targets throughout. Safe default.
- **Front-load** — higher intake early, taper later. Useful for races where late-stage stomach issues are likely.
- **Taper** — lower early, ramp later. Less common; useful for races with an easier opening.

Per-segment overrides let you tighten or loosen targets on specific stretches — hot exposed segments need more fluid, technical climbs need fewer gels because you can't open them while scrambling.

## Per-segment plan

For each segment, the planner shows:

- **Time to next checkpoint** — based on your pace plan.
- **What you need** — calories, carbs, fluid, sodium for that segment.
- **What you carry** — the planner adds up gel/bar/drink mix counts based on your targets.
- **What you grab at the next aid station** — refills and restock for the segment after.

The numbers cross-check against the [Gear](/wizard/gear) step's carry capacity. If a segment needs more fluid than you can hold, the planner flags it.

## Per-checkpoint refills

For each aid-station checkpoint, plan what you'll do:

- **Refill flasks** — how much, what mix.
- **Grab food** — gels, bars, real food (only what the [route checkpoints](/routes/checkpoints) say is actually available).
- **Pack snacks** — what goes into your vest for the next segment.

The mobile app shows this as a checklist on race day. Tap each item as you complete it so you don't forget the salt tabs while you're fumbling with your flask.

## Fueling rules

For long races, set if-then rules that fire automatically based on conditions:

- *If* hours into the race ≥ 8, *then* switch from gels to real food.
- *If* segment is hot (>25°C), *then* add 200ml fluid.
- *If* climbing, *then* slow carb intake to avoid GI distress.

Rules show up as reminders in the mobile app as conditions trigger.

<Tip>
  Don't go above your trained intake on race day. The plan is only as good as what your gut can handle — test the targets on long training runs first.
</Tip>
