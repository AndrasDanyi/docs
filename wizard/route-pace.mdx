This is the foundation of the plan — everything that comes after (checkpoints, gear, fuel, drop bags, tasks) is built around the route and pace you set here.

## Pick a route

Three options at the top of the step:

  <Card title="Saved route" icon="map">
    Pick from routes in your library — the most common path.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Upload GPX" icon="upload">

  <Card title="Draw route" icon="pencil">

## Target finish time

Two ways to set it:

- **Type the time directly** — `12:00:00` for a 12-hour goal.
- **Pick from suggestions** — the planner offers conservative, realistic, and ambitious targets based on the route's distance and elevation. Useful when you don't have a strong opinion yet.

The target propagates to every later step: fueling rates per hour, gear logistics that depend on when you hit each checkpoint, cutoff risk flags on the checkpoints step.

## Pacing strategy

Pick how effort is distributed across the race:

- **Even** — same effort throughout. Safe default.
- **Negative split** — easier early, harder late. Classic ultra strategy.
- **Front-loaded** — harder early, easier late. Useful for races where you want a buffer against cutoffs.

You can override the strategy per segment from the checkpoints step if a single strategy doesn't fit the whole course.

## Segment effort dials

For each segment, three sliders adjust effort relative to your overall strategy:

- **Uphill effort** — how hard you push on climbs.
- **Downhill effort** — how hard you push on descents.
- **Flat effort** — how hard you push on runnable flats.

The planner converts these into per-segment splits and shows the resulting paces. If the split feels wrong (too fast on a technical descent, for example), pull the slider back.

## What you'll see

A timetable of segments with predicted splits, cumulative time at each checkpoint, and total finish time. If a cutoff is at risk, the planner flags it with a warning.

<Tip>
  Don't over-tune segment effort on your first pass. Lock in route + target finish + overall strategy, then look at the timetable. Only adjust segments if a specific split looks unrealistic.
</Tip>
