The Gear step has three tabs because gear planning has three distinct questions: *what do I carry between checkpoints*, *what does the race require I always have*, and *what changes at each stop*.

## Carry & Mandatory tab

### Carry setup

Pick the pack or vest you'll race in, the number of flasks or bladder capacity, and where each is distributed. The planner uses this to know how much fluid and food you can hold between aid stations.

The Fuel step cross-checks: if you've planned 800ml of drink per hour but you can only carry 500ml between aid stations 90 minutes apart, the planner flags the gap.

### Mandatory gear

The race's required equipment list (UTMB, the major US ultras, most European mountain ultras). Pick items from your [Gear library](/gear/overview) — they have to match by type (e.g., the race needs a "waterproof jacket with sealed seams", your library has one tagged that way).

If you don't see the right race in the dropdown, switch to **Custom checklist** and add items manually. Useful for unsupported events or your own personal "I never start without this" list.

## Logistics tab

This is where per-checkpoint gear changes live. For each checkpoint with crew or drop bag access, you can plan:

- **What to swap** — fresh shirt at km 50, socks at km 80.
- **What to pick up** — headlamp before sunset, jacket before the cold descent.
- **What to drop off** — heavy pack contents you no longer need.

The mobile app fires reminders at each checkpoint listing exactly what you planned to swap, pick up, and drop.

### Linked products

If a gear item is linked to a product (you set this up in the Gear library), the planner shows the product details — brand, model, weight, link to the product page. Useful when a race or brand partner wants athletes to use specific gear.

## What the Fuel step uses from here

- **Pack and flask capacity** — sets the upper bound on what you can carry between checkpoints.
- **Carry distribution** — tells the planner whether your flasks are accessible mid-run or need a stop.

The two steps are coupled — change your pack here and the Fuel step's "what to carry" math updates automatically.
